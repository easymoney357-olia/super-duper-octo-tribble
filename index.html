Question 1

The recent trade war between China and US has prompted many companies to rethink their offshore financing activities. A Chinese construction company, Blue Sky (BS) is considering issuing a fixed rate Kangaroo bond to raise AUD 200 million for its growth in the next five years. Oceana Gas (OG), an energy company in Australia, is looking to raise a Hong Kong Dollar debt due to its recent successful expansion in China. OG would like to borrow via a fixed rate loan of HKD 900 million.

BS and OG's current borrowing costs are below:

CompanyBSOG
HKD4%6%
AUD7%6.8%
Desired amountHKD 900mAUD 200m

Spot rate (mid-rate) of AUD: HKD/AUD 4.5

Quotes for 5-year currency swap (assuming annual payment):

  • 6.8%–6.9% against 1-year USD SOFOR for AUD
  • 4%–5.5% against 1-year USD SOFOR for HKD
A. Please recommend a borrowing strategy for both BS and OG using the comparative advantage argument. Discuss how the swap arrangement will help each company save and borrow the needed currencies. Outline the positions of BS, OG, and the dealer bank under a swap arrangement. Describe in detail the cost of savings for both companies. Compute the possible profits in PERCENTAGES for the swap bank.
B. Calculate the annual cash flows from year 0 – year 5 for the company BS in the swap contract.

Question 3

A multinational investment bank's foreign exchange trading desk is monitoring currency markets for potential arbitrage opportunities. A trader notices the following spot exchange rates quoted simultaneously by different dealers:

  • 1 USD = 0.85 EUR
  • 1 EUR = 140 JPY
  • 1 USD = 115 JPY

The trader has access to USD 1,000,000 and can execute transactions instantly without transaction costs.

Required:

A. Determine whether a triangular arbitrage opportunity exists. If so, show the sequence of currency exchanges the trader should undertake.
B. Calculate the final amount of USD obtained after completing the arbitrage cycle and determine the arbitrage profit.
C. Explain how triangular arbitrage helps enforce the Law of One Price in foreign exchange markets. What will happen to the exchange rates if many traders exploit this opportunity simultaneously?
